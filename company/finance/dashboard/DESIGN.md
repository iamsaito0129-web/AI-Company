# Notion Design Guidelines (DESIGN.md)

## 1. Overview
Notion's design is characterized by its minimalism, clarity, and "digital paper" aesthetic. It balances high functionality with a clean, approachable interface that focuses on content and collaboration.

## 2. Visual Principles
- **Clarity over Clutter:** Use whitespace generously to separate ideas and elements.
- **Content First:** The UI should recede, letting the user's data and content take center stage.
- **Human-Centric:** Incorporate playful elements like emojis and custom illustrations to soften the technical nature of the tool.
- **Systematic Structure:** Use consistent grids, borders, and shadows to create a clear hierarchy.

## 3. Foundation
### Typography
- **Primary Font:** Sans-serif (Inter or system fonts like Segoe UI, San Francisco).
- **Secondary Font:** Serif (Lyon-style fonts for a more "intellectual" or "published" look).
- **Scale:** High contrast between titles and body text, but minimal variation in body text sizes.

### Color Palette
- **Backgrounds:** Pure white (#FFFFFF) or very light gray (#F7F6F3) for secondary areas.
- **Text:** High-contrast dark gray/black (#37352F) for primary text, medium gray (#787774) for secondary text.
- **Accents:** Muted, purposeful colors for labels and highlights (e.g., light blue, yellow, green, pink). Avoid neon or overly vibrant shades.
- **Borders:** Subtle gray lines (#EDEDED) to define blocks and sections.

### Iconography & Illustrations
- **Icons:** Use simple, line-based icons (e.g., Feather icons style).
- **Emojis:** Used as page icons, category markers, and emotional signals.
- **Illustrations:** Black and white, hand-drawn style (reminiscent of dry-erase markers or woodblock prints).

## 4. Layout & UI Components
- **Sidebar:** Minimalist navigation with collapsible sections and clear active states.
- **Blocks:** Everything is a block. Elements should feel like they can be moved and rearranged.
- **Modals & Popovers:** Simple, rounded corners (3px to 5px), subtle box shadows.
- **Buttons:** Low-profile buttons with subtle hover states. Primary actions are clear but not overpowering.

## 5. Interaction Design
- **Hover States:** Subtle background color changes to indicate interactivity.
- **Transitions:** Minimal, snappy animations that don't distract from the task.
- **Empty States:** Encouraging and clean, often featuring an emoji or a simple illustration.
