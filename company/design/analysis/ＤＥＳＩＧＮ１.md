# Design System Documentation: The Kinetic Classroom
 
## 1. Overview & Creative North Star
This design system is built to transform the standard classroom presentation into a "High-End Editorial" experience for 5th and 6th-grade students. Moving away from the static, boxy templates of traditional educational software, we embrace a **Creative North Star: The Kinetic Classroom.**
 
The goal is to create a digital environment that feels alive, sophisticated, and intentionally energetic. We achieve this through:
*   **Intentional Asymmetry:** Off-center layouts that leave breathing room for character interaction.
*   **Dynamic Overlap:** Elements (like typography and character placeholders) should break container boundaries to create a sense of three-dimensional space.
*   **High-Contrast Scale:** Using massive `display-lg` typography against tight, functional labels to guide the eye instantly to the most important learning point.
 
---
 
## 2. Colors & Surface Philosophy
The palette balances deep, focus-oriented blues (`primary`) with high-energy yellows and oranges (`tertiary` and `secondary`).
 
### The "No-Line" Rule
To maintain a premium, modern feel, **1px solid borders are strictly prohibited** for sectioning content. Boundaries must be defined through:
1.  **Background Color Shifts:** Use `surface-container-low` for secondary content areas sitting on a `surface` background.
2.  **Tonal Transitions:** Use subtle shifts in lightness to separate a sidebar from the main canvas.
 
### Surface Hierarchy & Nesting
Treat the UI as a series of stacked, physical layers.
*   **Base:** `surface` (#f8f5ff)
*   **Sections:** `surface-container-low` (#f2efff)
*   **Interactive Cards:** `surface-container-lowest` (#ffffff) to provide a "pop" of clarity.
*   **Floating Elements:** `surface-container-high` or `highest` for modals or temporary overlays.
 
### The "Glass & Gradient" Rule
Flat color is the enemy of energy. 
*   **Signature Gradients:** Apply a linear gradient from `primary` (#004be2) to `primary-container` (#809bff) at a 135-degree angle for hero buttons and key highlight areas.
*   **Glassmorphism:** For character dialogue bubbles or floating toolbars, use a semi-transparent `surface` color with a `backdrop-filter: blur(20px)`. This creates a "frosted glass" effect that keeps the classroom background visible but blurred, adding professional depth.
 
---
 
### 3. Typography
We use a dual-font strategy to balance excitement with legibility.
 
*   **The Action Font (Plus Jakarta Sans):** Used for `display`, `headline`, and `label` roles. Its geometric, open counters are friendly and highly readable from the back of a classroom.
*   **The Clarity Font (Be Vietnam Pro):** Used for `title` and `body` roles. Its clean, humanist proportions make longer English sentences easy for students to parse.
 
**Hierarchy as Identity:**
*   **Large-Scale English:** Use `display-lg` for the "Word of the Day" or "Key Question." High contrast in size communicates what the student *must* focus on.
*   **Subtle Instructions:** Use `label-md` in `on-surface-variant` for teacher-facing notes or secondary instructions.
 
---
 
## 4. Elevation & Depth
Depth is achieved through **Tonal Layering** and **Ambient Light**, never harsh drop shadows.
 
*   **The Layering Principle:** Instead of a shadow, place a `surface-container-lowest` card on top of a `surface-container` background. The subtle shift in hex code creates a natural "lift."
*   **Ambient Shadows:** For floating action buttons, use a shadow with a 24px-32px blur and 6% opacity. The shadow color must be a tinted version of `on-surface` (#2a2b51), not pure black.
*   **Ghost Borders:** If a boundary is required for accessibility, use a "Ghost Border" of `outline-variant` at 15% opacity. It should feel like a suggestion of a line, not a hard barrier.
 
---
 
## 5. Components
 
### Buttons (The Rounded Scale)
*   **Primary Button:** Uses the `xl` (3rem) or `full` (9999px) roundedness. Style with the Primary-to-Primary-Container gradient. 
*   **Secondary/Tertiary:** Use `surface-container-highest` background with `on-surface` text. 
*   **Interaction:** On hover, the button should scale up (1.05x) rather than just changing color.
 
### Character Stage (Placeholder Layouts)
*   **The "Anchor" Rule:** Character images should never be floating in the center of a box. They should be anchored to the bottom-right or bottom-left of the screen, partially overlapping the `surface-container` to break the "frame."
 
### Cards & Lists
*   **Forbid Divider Lines:** Use `1.5rem` (md) vertical spacing or background tonal shifts (e.g., alternating between `surface` and `surface-container-low`) to separate list items.
*   **Roundedness:** All cards must use `lg` (2rem) corner radius to maintain the friendly, approachable aesthetic.
 
### Input Fields (Learning Interaction)
*   **Visual Style:** High-affordance, rounded `md` (1.5rem) containers.
*   **Focus State:** Instead of a thin line, use a 3px "glow" using the `primary_fixed` color at 40% opacity.
 
---
 
## 6. Do’s and Don’ts
 
### Do
*   **Do** overlap typography and images. Let the "E" in a large English word be partially obscured by a character's head to create depth.
*   **Do** use `tertiary_container` (#fdd400) for "Success" or "Bonus" moments to spark joy and energy.
*   **Do** allow for generous white space. High-end design is defined by what you *don't* fill.
 
### Don't
*   **Don't** use 100% black text. Always use `on-surface` (#2a2b51) to keep the UI soft and integrated.
*   **Don't** use square corners. Nothing in this system should have a radius smaller than `sm` (0.5rem).
*   **Don't** use standard slide transitions (like "Push" or "Wipe"). Use "Smart Animate" style transitions where elements scale and slide into place independently.
 
---
 
## 7. Animation Principles
Animation should feel **"Snappy yet Elastic."**
*   **Entry:** Elements should enter with a slight "overshoot" (back-out easing). 
*   **Staggering:** When showing a list of vocabulary, stagger the entry of each item by 0.05s to create a "waterfall" effect.
*   **Meaningful Motion:** Characters should have a subtle "floating" idle animation (Y-axis translation of 4px) to keep the screen feeling alive during long explanations.```
